01 · Foundations

About Cricket

A bat-and-ball sport played between two teams of eleven, cricket blends strategy, skill, and endurance into a game loved by billions across the world.

📜

History

Cricket originated in England, evolving from a children's pastime in the 16th century into a structured sport governed by formal laws by the 18th century.

🏏

What is Cricket

A game played between two teams of eleven players, using a bat and ball, where one team bats to score runs while the other bowls and fields to restrict them.

🌍

Origin

Believed to have started in South-East England, cricket spread across the British Empire and is now a major sport in countries like India, Australia, and the Caribbean.

🎯

Objective

The objective is simple — score more runs than the opposition while dismissing their batters within the allotted overs or innings.

🧑‍🤝‍🧑

Players

Each team fields eleven players, split into batters, bowlers, all-rounders, and a wicketkeeper, each with a specialised role.

👨‍⚖️

Umpires

Two on-field umpires enforce the laws of the game, make decisions on dismissals and deliveries, and are supported by a third umpire using technology.

🟫

Pitch

A 22-yard strip of carefully prepared turf at the centre of the ground where the bowler delivers the ball and the batter defends the wicket.

🪙

Toss

Before the match, captains toss a coin to decide which team will bat or bowl first, a decision often shaped by pitch and weather conditions.

🔄

Innings

An innings is the period during which one team bats. Matches can have one or two innings per side depending on the format.

⏱️

Overs

An over consists of six legal deliveries bowled by a single bowler from one end of the pitch, after which a new bowler takes over.

🏃

Runs

Runs are scored by batters running between the wickets, or automatically when the ball crosses the boundary after touching the ground or in the air.

🚧

Boundaries

The edge of the field of play — hitting the ball over it on the bounce scores four runs, and over it in the air scores six runs.

02 · Glossary

Cricket Basics

Get familiar with the essential terms and equipment that form the building blocks of every cricket match.

🟫

Pitch

The rectangular strip, 22 yards long, where most of the game's action between bat and ball takes place.

📏

Crease

White lines marked on the pitch that define safe areas for batters and the bowler's legal delivery zone.

🏏

Bat

A flat wooden implement, traditionally made of willow, used by batters to hit the ball and score runs.

🔴

Ball

A hard, leather-covered sphere with a raised seam, weighing around 156-163 grams in international cricket.

🥢

Wicket

Three wooden stumps topped with two bails, which the bowler aims to hit to dismiss the batter.

🚧

Boundary

The perimeter of the field, marked by a rope or line, that determines four and six-run scoring shots.

🏃‍♂️

Runs

The basic unit of scoring, earned by running between the wickets or hitting the ball to the boundary.

➕

Extras

Runs added to a team's total that aren't scored off the bat — including wides, no-balls, byes and leg byes.

⚡

Powerplay

Designated overs at the start (and sometimes middle) of an innings with fielding restrictions to encourage attacking play.

🛡️

Maiden Over

An over in which no runs are scored off the bat, considered a strong show of bowling discipline.

⚫

Dot Ball

A delivery from which no runs are scored, helping bowlers build pressure on the batting side.

🔁

Over

A set of six consecutive legal deliveries bowled by one bowler from the same end of the pitch.

03 · Laws of the Game

Cricket Rules

Click any card to expand and learn how each rule shapes the way the game is played and decided.

🚷

LBW

+

Leg Before Wicket. A batter is out if the ball would have hit the stumps but is intercepted by any part of their body (usually the leg), provided other conditions like pitching line and impact are met.

↔️

Wide

+

A delivery bowled too far from the batter for them to reasonably play a normal cricket shot. It results in one extra run and an additional ball to be bowled.

🚫

No Ball

+

An illegal delivery, often due to the bowler overstepping the crease. It awards one run, an extra ball, and in limited-overs cricket usually comes with a free hit.

➰

Bye

+

Runs scored when the ball passes the batter without touching the bat or body and the batters run, usually due to the wicketkeeper missing it.

🦵

Leg Bye

+

Runs scored when the ball strikes the batter's body (not the bat) and the batters choose to run between the wickets.

🏃‍♂️💨

Run Out

+

A batter is dismissed if a fielder breaks the wicket with the ball while the batter is outside their crease while attempting a run.

🧤

Stumping

+

The wicketkeeper dismisses the batter by breaking the wicket with the ball while the batter is out of their crease, usually while playing or missing a delivery.

💥

Hit Wicket

+

A batter is out if they accidentally dislodge the bails or break the wicket with their bat or body while playing a shot or setting off for a run.

⏸️

Dead Ball

+

A delivery considered out of play, often called by the umpire, where no runs can be scored and no dismissal can occur.

🆓

Free Hit

+

A bonus delivery awarded after certain no-balls in limited-overs cricket, where the batter cannot be dismissed except by run out.

📉

Follow On

+

In Test cricket, a team that trails by a large margin after the first innings can be asked to bat again immediately by the opposing captain.

⚔️

Super Over

+

A tie-breaker used in limited-overs cricket where each team bats for one additional over to determine the winner.

05 · Mastery

Batting

Great batting blends technique and timing. Here are the fundamentals and the shots every batter should know.

Fundamentals

✋

Grip

How the hands are placed on the bat handle, balancing control and power for different shots.

🧍

Stance

The batter's set-up position before the ball is delivered, providing balance and readiness to move.

⬆️

Backlift

The upward movement of the bat before the downswing, generating timing and power for the shot.

⚖️

Balance

Maintaining stable body weight distribution throughout the shot for control and consistency.

👟

Footwork

Quick, precise foot movements that get the body into the best position to play each delivery.

Shots

🏏

Cover Drive

An elegant shot played through the covers with a full extension of the arms and a high elbow.

➡️

Straight Drive

A classic shot hit straight back past the bowler with a vertical bat and full follow-through.

↩️

Pull

A horizontal-bat shot played to a short ball, rotating the body to send it through the leg side.

🪝

Hook

An aggressive shot played to a short, fast delivery aimed at the batter's head or chest, hit over fine leg.

🧹

Sweep

A horizontal shot played on one knee to a fuller-length ball, sweeping it to the leg side.

🔄

Reverse Sweep

A high-risk variation of the sweep where the batter switches hands to hit against the field's setup.

✂️

Cut

A horizontal-bat shot played to a short, wide ball, guiding it to the off side using the pace of the ball.

⬆️✂️

Upper Cut

A controlled shot played over the slip cordon to a short, fast ball outside off stump.

🪶

Flick

A wristy shot that uses minimal bat swing to deflect the ball, often played off the pads.

🚀

Lofted Shot

A shot played in the air over the infield, often used to clear the boundary for a six.

06 · Mastery

Bowling

From searing yorkers to mystery spin, bowling variations are designed to deceive and dismiss the batter.

Fast Bowling

🎯

Yorker

A full-length delivery aimed at the batter\'s feet or the base of the stumps, hard to dig out.

⚡

Bouncer

A short, fast delivery that rises sharply towards the batter's chest or head, testing reflexes.

↘️

Inswing

A delivery that curves in the air towards the batter, moving from off side to leg side for a right-hander.

↗️

Outswing

A delivery that curves away from the batter, moving from leg side to off side, often inducing edges.

🐢

Slower Ball

A deceptive delivery bowled with disguised lower pace to catch the batter off guard mid-shot.

🔪

Cutter

A delivery cut across the seam to change the ball's pace and movement off the pitch.

🌀

Reverse Swing

Late, sharp movement in the opposite direction to conventional swing, usually with an older ball at high pace.

Spin Bowling

🎡

Off Spin

Delivered by a finger spinner, turning the ball from off side to leg side for a right-hand batter.

🔁

Leg Spin

Delivered by a wrist spinner, turning the ball from leg side to off side for a right-hand batter.

🕵️

Googly

A leg-spinner's deceptive delivery that turns the opposite way to a normal leg break.

🎭

Doosra

An off-spinner's variation that turns away from a right-hander, the opposite of a standard off break.

🤏

Carrom Ball

A delivery flicked out using the thumb and middle finger, producing sharp, unpredictable turn.

🪂

Flipper

A fast, flat delivery that skids on after pitching, often deceiving the batter on bounce.

🔝

Top Spinner

A delivery that bounces higher than expected due to forward-rotating spin, with little sideways turn.

07 · Mastery

Fielding

Sharp fielding saves runs and creates wickets — the skills that separate good teams from great ones.

🧤

Catching

Cleanly taking the ball after it's hit in the air to dismiss the batter, requiring focus and safe hands.

🎯

Throwing

Accurately returning the ball to the wicketkeeper or bowler's end, often to prevent runs or effect run outs.

🛝

Sliding

A technique used to stop the ball quickly while running at speed, cutting off boundaries.

🤾

Diving

A full-stretch effort to stop or catch the ball, often the difference between a boundary and a single.

🎯💥

Direct Hit

Hitting the stumps directly with a throw from the field to run out a batter without the keeper's help.

🔁

Relay Throw

A quick two-stage throw between fielders to cover distance faster and prevent extra runs.

🏃‍♀️

Backing Up

A fielder positioning behind the stumps to collect any overthrow and prevent additional runs.

08 · The Game, Reimagined

Formats of Cricket

Cricket is played in several formats, each offering a different balance of strategy, endurance and entertainment.

5 DAYS

Test Cricket

  • Overs: Unlimited
  • Innings: 2 per side
  • Duration: Up to 5 days
  • Focus: Patience & technique
50 OVERS

ODI

  • Overs: 50 per side
  • Innings: 1 per side
  • Duration: ~8 hours
  • Focus: Balanced strategy
20 OVERS

T20

  • Overs: 20 per side
  • Innings: 1 per side
  • Duration: ~3 hours
  • Focus: Power-hitting
10 OVERS

T10

  • Overs: 10 per side
  • Innings: 1 per side
  • Duration: ~90 minutes
  • Focus: Fast-paced entertainment

09 · Communication on the Field

Umpire Signals

Umpires use a set of standard hand signals to communicate decisions to the scorers and players.

🙌

Four

Umpire waves an open hand from side to side at chest height to signal a boundary along the ground.

🙆

Six

Both arms raised straight above the head to signal the ball cleared the boundary without bouncing.

↔️

Wide

Both arms extended horizontally to indicate a delivery has been bowled out of the batter's reach.

🤲

No Ball

One arm extended horizontally and held out to the side to signal an illegal delivery.

☝️

Out

The index finger raised above the head, the most iconic signal, confirming a batter's dismissal.

🙇

Bye

One arm raised straight above the head, open palm forward, to signal runs scored as byes.

🦵🙋

Leg Bye

One leg tapped lightly with the hand while the other arm is raised, signalling leg byes.

✋

Dead Ball

Both arms crossed and uncrossed in front of and below the waist to signal the ball is out of play.

⏱️

Short Run

The umpire taps their shoulder with their hand to signal that a batter failed to reach the crease before turning.

10 · Gearing Up

Equipment

From the bat to the bails, every piece of cricket equipment serves a vital purpose in protecting players and shaping play.

🏏

Bat

A flat willow-wood blade used to strike the ball, available in various weights and shapes for different playing styles.

🔴

Ball

A leather sphere with a stitched seam, coloured red, white or pink depending on the format being played.

⛑️

Helmet

Protective headgear with a grille, essential for batters and close-in fielders against fast deliveries.

🦵

Pads

Leg guards worn by batters and wicketkeepers to protect the shins and knees from impact.

🧤

Gloves

Padded gloves that protect a batter's hands and fingers while gripping the bat.

👟

Shoes

Spiked cricket shoes that provide grip on grass during batting, bowling and fielding.

🛡️

Guard

An abdominal protector worn beneath clothing to shield against impacts from fast deliveries.

🩳

Thigh Pad

Padding worn on the front leg's thigh to absorb impact from balls striking the body.

🦺

Chest Guard

Protective padding worn under the shirt to shield the chest area from short, fast deliveries.

🥢

Stumps

Three vertical wooden posts that form the wicket, which the bowler aims to hit or dislodge.

➖

Bails

Two small wooden pieces resting on top of the stumps, whose dislodgement can signal a dismissal.
